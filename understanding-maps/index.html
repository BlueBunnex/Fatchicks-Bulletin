Introduction

The term pedophile is often misused in common media to refer to someone who sexually abuses children, but this oversimplification mischaracterizes many people who, while attracted to children, have never acted or even vehemently disapprove of their attraction. Among other things, I want to discuss a number of things people get wrong about pedophilia:

  • Being a pedophile does not mean someone has sexually abused children.
  • Someone who sexually abuses children is not necessarily a pedophile.
  • Pedophilia is a treatable mental disorder.
  • Many pedophiles don't want to sexually abuse children, want to get help, but are too scared to do so for fear of backlash from admitting they're attracted to children.

In honest, pedophilia is a taboo subject, which is part of the reason why people object so vehemently any discussion of pedophilia as something other than an immoral crime. As a psychological disorder, pedophilia that requires treatment.

Pedophilia is a form of paraphilia. Because it causes harm to others, it is considered a disorder. Doctors diagnose pedophilia when people feel greatly distressed or become less able to function well because of their attraction to children or when they have acted on their urges. This is to say, a person attracted to minors that has no trouble resisting their desires is not considered as having Pedophilic Disorder.

Pedophilia, especially in predatory pedophiles, often cooccurs with other disorders like antisocial personality disorder, substance use disorder, depression, and so forth, emphasizing the need for therapy and treatment.
