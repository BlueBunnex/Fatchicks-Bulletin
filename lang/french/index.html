Compound past

Present auxiliary verb + unique verb form

The compound past has the same meaning as the imperfect but is more archaic.

There are two auxiliary verbs to indicate the past tense — which auxiliary verb is used for which verb must be memorized.

Some verbs use avoir (infinitive, to have).

J'ai envoyé -- I sent

Tu as envoyé -- You sent

Tu m'as envoyé -- You sent (to) me

Il/elle a envoyé -- He/she/it sent

Other verbs use être (infinitive, to be).

Je suis parti -- I left

Tu es parti -- You left

Il/elle est parti -- He/she/it left

Pluperfect (past in past)

Imperfect auxiliary verb + unique verb form

The only difference from the compound past is the tense of the auxiliary verb, which changed from present to imperfect. This is why the pluperfect "happens earlier" than the compound past.

J'avais envoyé -- I had sent

en tant que

en tant que is similar to comme, and often both are translated as "as". However, there's a difference:

en tant que /ɑ̃ tɑ̃ kə/ -- (literally) being, in the position that (of)

En tant que chat j'aime le poisson. -- Being a cat, I love fish.

Seems to come historically from "in the capacity that (of), inasmuch that (of), insofar that (of)"

autant que -- as much as [quantity]

Autant que chat j'aime le poisson. -- As much as a cat, I love fish.
